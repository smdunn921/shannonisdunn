The Lonely Months Project

On the Days When I Can't Think of Myself, I Think About You Instead



The Lonely Months Project is an ongoing project for my Digital Storytelling 2 class, in which I take poems I've written and turn them into visual poems. My current goal is to have four by the end of the semester. The Lonely Months itself is a mental space– my mental space– for when I have to dig myself back out of the hole I've found myself in, sometimes with help, sometimes without. Most of these poems are written about and for other times, places, and people, all of which and whom I find myself missing at any given moment.

I think it's especially fun to note the contrast between this and my project for Digital Storytelling 1, which was titled Beauty is Hidden All Around Us. In that project, I tried to look for beauty in the small things. In this project, I am allowing myself the space for the sadness that comes with the realization that life sucks sometimes. Either way, isn't life wonderful in the way we are able to have these different perspectives? I hope this new path I'm pursuing allows me room to grow, both as a writer and as a human.
