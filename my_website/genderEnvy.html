gender envy

Oh to be covered
in dirt and ivy
with moss dusted
over my limbs.
To be a void,
a pool of smokey
blackness,
moving freely.
I’m envious of
genders that
don’t exist outside
of my thoughts.
Trapped in this
body that doesn’t
feel like it should
host my soul.
But for now I’ll
cut my hair again
and silently nod,
as I pretend I love
humanity.
